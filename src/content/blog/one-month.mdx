## The Big Glow-up? 🤔

*"But Vignesh, what even changed?!"*, you might ask...

Well...everything, kind of. Let's start with the most obvious one.

If you visited the blog anywhere close to when I launched it, you'd have seen a very different site. It was this sort of blueish-green website, with green accents...

Pretty loud to be honest.

I didn't necessarily hate it, but one day, I stumbled upon [WerWolv's portfolio](https://werwolv.net/) and something about it just clicked. The grid structure and the slanted dividers is what caught my attention. It had sort of an industrial vibe to it that I liked. I then checked out [Better-Auth's website](https://better-auth.com/) and I absolutely <span class="text-pink-400 font-bold uppercase">loved</span> the design of it!

The old theme suddenly felt like it was a bit too much, so, I then decided to go for a full theme revamp.

## Font Toggle

This one's a small but fun addition.

I added a font toggle button to the blog (*you might've already noticed it*).

It lets you switch between the default monospace font and a serif one. Sometimes I'm just in the mood for a good serif aesthetic, and I was like, ***why not let the reader choose it too?***

A pretty small change, but it makes the reading experience feel a little bit more personal.

---
## RSS Support

For all the feed nerds out there, the blog now has RSS support!

If you don't know what RSS is, it basically lets you subscribe to websites and get updates in a feed reader, without needing to check every site manually.

I personally use [feeeed](https://feeeed.nateparrott.com/) on **iOS**. It has a really clean UI.

However, if you're a self-hosting *nerd*, you could self-host an instance of [FreshRSS](https://freshrss.org/index.html).

Either way, the blog shows up cleanly in both.

You can find the RSS feed link in the navbar. Go subscribe! 🙂

## Callouts

Okay, now the thing I've been excited to talk about... callouts!

If you've read my previous post, you might've already seen these. They're little styled blocks I can drop into any post to highlight something. The inspiration to make these came from reading [Josh W Comeau's](https://www.joshwcomeau.com/) blog post - ["How I Built My Blog"](https://www.joshwcomeau.com/blog/how-i-built-my-blog-v2/). *(You should go check it out as well! It's an interesting read.)*

They could be a tip, a fun note, a "wait, hold on" moment.

They give the page some much needed vibe, and honestly, it makes writing more fun.
